About Me

I've spent the last 10 years using technology to bring people together in various attempts to make the world a better place.

That's meant everything from helping to elect a President to founding a venture-backed startup.

Along the way, I've had a plenty of roles: leading large teams, managing software development, heading up marketing, fundraising, creating analytics products and directing campaigns to name a few.

But, in the end, the thing I always love the most is working with a group of talented people to build something that matters.

A few of the highlights (head over to LinkedIn for the full resume):

  • At MoveOn, I developed a new approach to building a field network: peer-to-peer organizing, backed up by modern web tools. With a small team, we were able to build a national, volunteer-driven network that was capable of matching the rapid pace of online campaigns.
  • I also helped to design and run MoveOn's 2006 and 2008 electoral campaigns. Through these campaigns we played a huge role in taking back the House and the Senate, and electing President Obama.
  • As the Founding Director at New Media Ventures, I launched the country's first network of progressive angel investors and helped to mainstream the idea that you can work to create political change, and have a for-profit business model.
  • Most recently, I headed up marketing and operations as co-founder of Scoot Networks, the world's first network of shared electric scooters. I helped the organization grow from the germ of an idea to a fully operational network with electric scooters parked all around San Francisco and thousands of happy customers. I'm incredibly proud of the work our team continues to.
  • I've also had the privilege to serve as an early advisor to some amazing do-gooder startups like Upworthy and Ethical Electric.

I live in San Francisco with the lovely Natalie Foster (@nataliefoster) and our baby boy.

About This Site

I build this site from scratch as part of an effort to increase my basic web skills. It's fully repsonsive and made with hand coded HTML and CSS, with a little bit of javascript and jQuery thrown in for good measure. It's hosted on GitHub, along with an easy-to-use template for creating personal sites like this one.

The blog is a Jekyl template that I borrowed almost entirely from Barry Clark's Jekyll Now project--which is an awesome resource if you're looking to put together a simple Jekyl blog.

Most icons are from the Noun Project. Thanks to Ash N for the wrench and TukTuk Design for the profile icon.
